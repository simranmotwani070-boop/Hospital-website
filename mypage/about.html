A hospital is a healthcare institution designed to provide patient treatment using specialized medical and nursing staff and advanced medical equipment. Beyond treatment, modern hospitals act as vital hubs for preventive care, medical research, and the education of healthcare professionals. Core Functions
The primary goal of any hospital is to manage patient health through several key actions:
Patient Care: Delivering immediate medical attention for acute illnesses, severe injuries, and life-threatening conditions.
Diagnosis: Using laboratory tests, imaging (like MRIs and CT scans), and pathology to identify diseases.
Treatment & Surgery: Administering medications and performing complex operations to manage medical conditions.
Rehabilitation: Helping patients recover functional abilities after an illness or surgery through therapies like physical or speech therapy.
